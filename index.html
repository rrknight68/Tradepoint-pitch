The core trade-off
Splitting the $8.35M raise into a $4M senior pref tranche at 12% and a $4.1M Co-GP equity tranche pushes the equity IRR from 24.0% → 35.4% and the equity multiple from 1.74× → 2.19×. The GP gives up just $103,333 in profit split to make this happen — noise on a $9.92M profit project. The pref tranche is ideal for Pedro and existing LPs converting their notes at a higher rate than their current 8%.

Pref Tranche Strategy — Who Fills It

First move
Pedro converts his note
Instead of paying Pedro $680K at close, offer him a conversion into the pref tranche at 12% — up from his current 8% note rate. He earns more, stays senior in the structure, and you don't need to raise that $680K from outside capital. This alone fills 17% of the pref tranche.
Second move
Existing LPs convert to pref
Rather than cashing out 20 LPs at $1.6M, offer a conversion into the 12% pref tranche. They earn 50% more than their current 8% note rate, get a senior position, and you preserve that $1.6M for the equity raise. If Pedro + LPs both convert, the entire $4M pref tranche is filled from existing relationships.
Result
Outside raise drops to $4.1M
If the pref tranche is filled internally, your outside pitch becomes a clean $4.1M Co-GP equity position at 35.4% IRR and 2.19×. That is a fundamentally different fundraising conversation than $8.35M at 24%.
GP economics
Barely changes
GP total economics under Option B: $5.07M (split + dev fee + mgmt fees). Under Option A: $5.18M. The $103K difference is the entire cost of pushing the equity IRR 11 points higher. Take the trade every time.
